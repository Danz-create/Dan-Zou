**Role:** Study design lead, sensor coordination, participant support  
**Setting:** Guangzhou, China (*n* = 20)

## Overview

A multi-modal field study collecting synchronized GPS trajectories, personal environmental sensor readings (noise, air pollution), and structured behavioral surveys from participants over an extended period, used to examine how daily activity patterns mediate environmental exposure and short-term health responses.

## Key contributions

- Designed and piloted the participant survey and data-collection protocol
- Coordinated hardware customization with sensor suppliers and built the data-handling pipeline
- Provided continuous participant support to achieve full completion of the study
- Data subsequently fed into several peer-reviewed publications on noise, air pollution, and mobility–health links
